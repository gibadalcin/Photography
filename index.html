About Us
Who we are?

Photography, when it first appeared in 19th century, used a few different technologies that existed for thousands of years. Chinese philosopher Mo Ti and Greek mathematicians Aristotle and Euclid knew about pinhole camera in the 5th century BC and Byzantine mathematician Anthemius of Tralles used a camera obscura in his experiments in 6th century. Pinhole camera is a box that has a pinhole aperture on one side and is completely dark inside. When light passes through a pinhole it projects an inverted image on a wall opposite of a pinhole. This is a simple camera and it has no lens. Some pinhole cameras can be found in nature (for instance in caves). Camera obscura works on a similar principle but it often has a lens, through which light passes, and a mirror which inverts and projects the image at the top of the box.
